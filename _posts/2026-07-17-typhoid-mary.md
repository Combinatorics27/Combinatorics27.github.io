mary was a cook in usa who carried the salmonella typhi bacteria but was asymptomatic. so she could spread typhoid without having the knowledge that she was.

after authorities figured it out, she was quarantined at the north brother island in new york for 3 years. she was then released but spread the bacteria again. the authorities then had her back at the island quarantined for 23 years until her death.

23 years on an island. i'm barely an year old than that duration. what humans have gone through is astonishing.

the island has now been abandoned. 

asymptomatic carriers pose an interesting ethical question - how do you balance individual freedom and social health in instances of asymptomatic carriers?
