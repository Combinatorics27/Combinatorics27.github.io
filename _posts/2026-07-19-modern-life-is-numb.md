war and physical violence has been a common part of human lives until only recently. we live in relatively very peaceful times now in the 21st century.

the characters in the movie fight club mention how normal life feels so numb and boring after they join the club. this is biologically true. during periods of violence, a person enters a different version of consciousness. most of their brain resources are diverted towards attention, and dopamine and opiods and endorphins are released causing the person to feel a 'alive'.

modern life then is really numbing. we used to fight in wars, now we get stressed my emails. which tells us about the adaptive features of the brain. 

the same brain could go on for years fighting wars and the same brain can get stressed about abstract deadlines. 

same brain, different environment. which points to how what you are currently is not unique. what you are has been shaped much by what the environment and the cultural norms you grew up in. you wouldn't be you if the environment was different.
