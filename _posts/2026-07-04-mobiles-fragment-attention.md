title: "thoughts: mobiles fragment attention"
date: 2026-07-04
categories: til
---

fifa world cup round of 32 has been insane. absolute cinema as they call it. i've been fortunate to watch some matches live. 

i watched a couple of matches in full attention - alone, mobile phone out of sight and all the norepinephrine allocated to the television. i experienced those matches. goosebumps are the biological proof.

i did watch argentina vs cabo verbe live as well. but unfortunately, i didn't experience it fully. because i was half-watching since i had my phone in hand. i did not give the match the norepinephrine it deserved. so while i can claim to have watched the match 'live', i cannot lie to myself. 

i'm afraid this might be the way many people watch matches now. tv or laptop turned on, but their attention on their mobiles and attention diverted to the match only to the cue of shouting commentators. 

i've learnt now. experiencing requires focused attention. fragmenting attention is really helpful if you want to pass time but its really a curse if you want to truly experience things. 

technology is double-edged.
