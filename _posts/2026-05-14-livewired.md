[Livewired](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/51778153-livewired) by David Eagleman.

- ***What we think of as you is a vessel of experience into which is poured a small sample of space and time. Who you are owes as much to your surroundings as it does to the DNA inside you.***
  - each of us individually has seen but a small percent of world but that is what our entire world model is based on. although if the world is compressible due to laws, then a small sample can suffice.

- ***Unconscious actions are more rapid than conscious deliberation.... It all just proceeds on its own, seemingly magically, because typing has become a part of your circutry.... The advantages are speed and energy efficiency.***
- ***The expert's brain has developed neural circuitry specific to soccer, allowing him to make his moves with surprisingly little brain acitivity.***
  - this is counter-intuitive. if you make a beginner play soccer against an expert, the brain doing more work is actually the beginner's. the expert's brain is using much less energy.

- ***In an enriched environemnt, branches [dendrites] grow more lavishly. In a deprived environment, branches shrivel.***
  - this is why childhood and schooling can make such a huge difference. human brains are especially plastic at that age.
 
- ***Humans have only about 20,000 genes.***
- ***...Even so, he took some of the isolated females and had them impregnated to see how these disturbed monkeys would interact with children of their own. The results were disastrous. The isolated monkeys were completely unable to raise children.***
- margin note - *Constraints. No one is sad about not going to mars. Indians are sad about not being able to go to foreign. Past indians must not have been sad.*
  - this is interesting. you can't be sad about not being able to do something that's not practically possible yet. but we might have future children's who'll be fighting with their parents about how their friends have visited mars multiple times but they haven't yet.
- ***Maps of the body are found where inputs enter the brain (somatosensory cortex) and outputs leave the brain (motor cortex). Areas with more detailed sensation, and those that are more finely controlled, command more real estate.***
  - the brain is magical is all i have to say.
- ***...The map of the body had reorganized. The homunculus still looked like a monkey, but a monkey without a right arm..... The brain adapts to the body plan. When a hand is amputed, neighbouring cortical territories move in to usurp the hand's previously held territory.....In fact, if you merely tie two fingers of your hand togethor - so they no longer operate independently, their cortical representation will eventually merge to a single area.***
-  ***...a typical neuron reaches out to touch ten thousand neighbours. the strenghts are based on timings...because of synchrony, they strengthen their bonds... they release more neurotransmitters, or set up more receptors to receive the neurotransmitters.***
-  ***the area that processes visual written language in the sighted is the same area that becomes active when the blind read braille.***
-  ***the older the brain, the less flexible it is for redeployment.***
  - neuroplasticity is a double edged sword. it makes learning easier but then in order to have an identity, you need to have a low enough plasticity.
- ***blind people simply have more brain territory devoted to the task of listening.***
- ***rem sleep is when dreaming occurs....major muscle groups become paralysed...this combination crafts the experience of dreaming: the invasion of the electrical waves into the occipital cortex makes the visual system active, while the muscular paralysis keeps the dreamer from acting on the experiences.***
- ***people who have been blind from birth experience no visual imagery in their dreams, but they do have other sensory experiences in the dreams.***
- ***the hippocampus and the prefrontal cortex are less active during rem sleep, and this presumably accounts for the difficulty in remembering our dreams.... infants spend half of their sleeping time in REM, adults spend only 10-20%.***
  - the author hypothesises that the reason we dream is to keep the visual cortex active and prevent other brain areas from taking over the visual cortex real estate. he hypothiseses that in an alien planet that does not have nights will have aliens that do not dream.
- ***sensory substitution - the brain is able to use information coming from the skin as if it were coming from the eyes..... sensory input is best learned when one can interact with the world.***
  - feedback is very important for learning.
- ***...inputs to the brain - photons at the eyes, air compression waves at the ears, pressure on the skin - are all converted into the common currency of electrical signals.***
- ***...the auditory cortex adjusted its circuitry to resemble the connections of the primary visual cortex....this tells us that the pattern of inputs determines the fate of the cortex. The brain dynamically wires itself to best represent (and eventually act upon) whatever data came swimming in.***
- ***we normally think of the toungue as a taste organ, but it is loaded with touch receptors, making it an excellent brain-interface machine.**
  - it is possible to 'see' with your tongue. because vision arises not in the eyes but in the brain. the brain is magical. reminds me of the book that i only read the first 15-20% of - an immense world. and the word 'umwelt'. that different animals while living in the same world experience the world drastically different. visible light is just a tiny percent of electromagnetic spectrum that evolution figured was most important for us.
- ***even though all the activity is at the surface of your sensory detectors, you perceive evertything as out there.***
  - the brain builds up a reality. which might not be the actual reality.
- ***....beyond sensory substitution: sensory enhancement. what if you could take the current senses and make them better, wider, faster?***
  - this is the futuristic idea that most excites me. i really wish this gets invented within my lifetime. the idea is that your brain builds up your reality. that if you could have brain devices, you should be able to experience anything and everything. think virtual reality but that you can experience everything and your brain cannot distinguish. you could be sitting at your bedroom and decide you want to have the best food. you would just wear your brain device. whatever you can imagine, the device will make your brain thinks its the reality. and the brain couldn't tell. anything you ever wish for, you can experience. maybe this will have an effect on neurotransmitters and hedonic adaptation might come into play. but if we have brain devices, surely we can figure out neurotransmitters as well. constant, endless euphoria.
- ***...the eyeborg's camera detects wavelenghts of light beyond the normal spectrum: ... he can perceive infrared and ultraviolet the way snakes and bees do.***
- ***plug in a new data stream, and the brain will figure out how to use it... We can extend ourselves beyond the customs of our inherited genetics.***
  - the author also suggests going beyond sensory substitution and sensory enhancement to the world of sensory addition.
- ***emotions simply reflect the meaning of the data to you, in the context of your goals and evolutionary pressures.***
  - its amazing how one thing can be so valuable to one person and so valueless to the other. or that the same thing can be valuable to you at one period and valueless during some other period. we're just dna-programmed bioloigcal creatures each with a brain thats tasked with thinking its the most important so it should be selfish.
- ***...parasitic twin in the womb: one twin doesn't make it and gets absorbed into the body of the healthier twin.***
  - its fascinating how so many processes need to happen with high accuracy for giving birth. its just magical this concept of birth. life emerges out of unalive things.
- ***[the brain] employs a schema (pattens to organise different categories of information)***
  - schematic learning is one way 'learning compounds' can actually be true. as you learn more things, it becomes easier and faster to learn new things because you can relate it with prior knowledge.
- locked-in syndrome is when the person is aware and conscious but cannot move their body.
- ***when the brain decides to shift a limb, a pattern of neural activity sends the motor command down data cables in the spinal cord, out to the peripheral nerves, and there the electrical signals are converted into the release of chemicals, which cause the muscle to contract.***
- ***..particpant thinks about moving his arm, and the signals (passed through machine learning algorithms) bypass the damaged spinal cord and jump to the muscle stimulator. the arm moves.***
- ***the motor and sensory systems are not independent but instead yoked in an unbroken cycle of feedback.***
- ***...asomatognosia, which translates to 'not knowing one's body'. in asomatognosia, a person is no longer able to control a limb. as a result, the patient will deny that the limb belongs to her and will sometimes insist that the limb belongs to someone else.***
  - margin note - the brain is capable of fooling itself perfectly.
  - its amazing how the brain can fool itself. in people with enlarged amygdalas, the brain interprets objects such as sticks/bananas to be guns instead. brain is a prediction machine. but prediction is based on your training data, i.e. your experiences. people with traumatic experiences will obviously be less trustworthy of people than those that have been raised in wealthy neighbourhoods. i've seen this to be true even with dogs. i spent a huge amount of time with stray dogs during high school and i've seen this with dogs as well. some dogs, due to traumatic childhood are so difficult to gain the trust of, whereas i've also met dogs that saw me the first time but immediately made me a friend for life.
