the brain is interconnected and complex. humans have 2 selves - remembering self and the experiencing self. this idea i borrow from daniel kahneman's 'thinking, fast and slow' book. which self one should optimise for or what the optimum ratio is, i don't know. 

but what's interesting is that many memories are linked to a cue. a smell, a visual, a combination of stimulus. its only when you experience those cues do you remember that memory. 

so this is another feature that deep, open-ended conversations have. they take you to parts of your brain that are not travelled otherwise. 

i remembered a memory today thanks to an unlikely cue i stumbled upon. what's worth considering is that there must be so many more memories that i might never recall because i might never experience those cues that the memory is linked to. 

the memory exists, physically in the constellation of neurons in my brain, but i may never travel upon them.

another related thought is that people tend to review their lives in their deathbeds. rightly so. but i think its flawed. flawed because the brain isn't capable of correctly reviewing life. because you can't remember everything. because you don't need to.
